Uncountable Counting is fine for things and people, but not for stuff, like grass mud butter bread Can you see why nouns like this are called ‘uncountable’? Try saying one mud, two muds, three Invisible Bulls and gates and hikers and mud are solid things you can see or touch. But there are some things you can’t see or touch: danger fear stupidity speed luck fun We call these words abstract nouns. Can you think of any more abstract nouns? Collective nouns are words for groups or sets of things: a party of hikers a herd of cattle a crowd of spectators a flock of sheep Collective nouns have plural forms too: herds of cattle flocks of sheep English has some peculiar collective nouns. Did you know that a collection of geese is often called a gaggle? See what other unusual collective nouns you can find. Always plural There are English nouns with no singular form.
