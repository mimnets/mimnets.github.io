Object Sometimes the verb has an object as well as a subject: Lucy beat Tim. Tim's car hit the cones. The object is who or what something happens to, like being beaten or getting hit. Complement In some sentences the verb is followed by a description of the subject: Tim looks angry. Lucy is the champion. This part is called the complement.
