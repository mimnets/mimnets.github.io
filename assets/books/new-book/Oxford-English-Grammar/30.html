Clauses Here is a new sentence. First you see it on its own, then with a clause added: Simon left the football under the bench. :; HlffjHK Phrases Let’s start with a plain, simple sentence that has no extra parts. Simon hid the secret documents. Here it is with an extra phrase added: Simon hid the secret documents inside an old football. Extra phrases don’t have to be added to the end of a sentence. For example: Simon left the football under the bench while he made a phone-call. A clause is another short sentence, with its own subject and verb. So, in the longer sentence, there are two clauses: Simon left the football under the bench + he made a phone call But instead of being written as two sentences, with a full stop between them, they are joined by the word while to make one big sentence. 28
