Words: players in a team Words in a sentence are like players in a team. Each one has a job to do and a position to play in Word classes To show what they do, words have names too: noun adjective pronoun conjunction verb adverb preposition The names are called word classes You can find them next to the headwords in your dictionary: throw verb (throws, throwing, threw, thrown) send something through the air ball noun (balls) a round object used in many games Batter MT Word classes are also called Parts of Speech In sentences words have jobs and positions, like the players do: Spectator noun adverb The next batter whacked the ball high into the air. adjective verb noun preposition noun There are players who can play in more than one position, and there are words that can be in more than one class.
